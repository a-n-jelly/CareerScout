# /negotiate — Offer Negotiation Protocol

Helps think through how to respond to an offer: whether to negotiate, what to counter, and how to frame it on the call or in writing.

---

## When to Use

Triggered automatically by `/update` when an offer is logged. Can also be called directly.

---

## What You Need

From `context/state.md` for the application:
- Offer details: base, bonus, equity, any other comp
- Role and level

Also useful:
- Your comp target (if set in the record)
- Any competing offers or timelines

If offer details aren't in the record yet, ask before proceeding.

---

## Execution

### Step 1 — Assess the offer

Before advising, form an independent view:
- How does base compare to the JD range (if listed) and Seattle market for senior PM?
- Is the equity meaningful given stage/size of company?
- Is the bonus structure guaranteed or performance-based?
- What's the total comp picture?

State your read clearly. Don't just reflect the numbers back — give a verdict: strong, fair, below market, or needs more information.

### Step 2 — Recommend whether to negotiate

Be direct:
- If the offer is below market or below your target: recommend negotiating, say what to push on
- If the offer is strong: say so, and note what (if anything) is worth a light ask
- If equity or bonus is unclear: recommend getting clarity before deciding

### Step 3 — Draft the response

Depending on preference (call vs. email), draft:

**For a call:**
A short script — what to say, how to open, how to frame the counter. Keep it conversational, not confrontational. One ask at a time unless there's a compelling reason to bundle.

**For an email:**
A concise, warm email. Express genuine enthusiasm for the role first — then make the ask clearly and without over-explaining. End by making it easy for them to come back to you.

---

## Tone Rules

- Confident, not combative
- Grateful for the offer without being gushing about it
- Never apologise for negotiating
- No em-dashes
- British spellings

---

## Output

1. Your read on the offer (2–3 sentences)
2. Recommendation: negotiate / accept / get clarity first
3. Draft response (call script or email)
4. Ask: "Does this feel right, or do you want to push harder / softer on anything?"
