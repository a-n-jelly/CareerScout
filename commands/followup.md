# /followup — Post-Interview Follow-Up Email Protocol

Drafts a follow-up email after an interview. Should feel personal and considered — not a generic "thank you for your time."

---

## When to Use

Triggered automatically by `/update` after a completed interview is logged. Can also be called directly.

Send within 24 hours of the interview.

---

## What You Need

- Which role and company
- Who you spoke with (name and title if known)
- One or two specific things from the conversation worth referencing

If any of these are missing, ask for them before drafting — a generic follow-up is worse than none.

---

## Execution

1. Pull the application record from `context/state.md` for context
2. Ask (if not already known): "Who did you speak with, and is there anything specific from the conversation you want to reference?"
3. Draft the email:
   - **To:** interviewer by first name
   - **Subject:** short and specific — not "Following up on our conversation"
   - **Opening:** reference something real from the conversation — a problem they mentioned, a product direction, something they said that resonated
   - **Middle:** one sentence connecting your background to what came up — don't rehash the whole interview
   - **Close:** warm, forward-looking, one sentence. No "I look forward to hearing from you."
   - **Sign-off:** Kind Regards, Anjali

---

## Tone Rules

- Direct and warm — not effusive
- Sounds like you, not like a template
- No "I wanted to reach out to thank you" — just start with the substance
- No em-dashes
- British spellings

---

## Output

Draft the email in full. Then ask: "Anything you want to adjust — tone, what's referenced, length?"
